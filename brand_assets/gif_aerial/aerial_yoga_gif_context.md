## BRAND

### Colours
| Name | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Primary Teal | `#04A19C` | CTAs, links, key highlights |
| Deep Teal | `#0C5F5C` | Headlines, navigation, dark backgrounds |
| Soft Teal | `#8DB8B3` | Borders, captions, supporting UI |
| Warm Sand | `#D9CBB8` | Section backgrounds, card fills |
| Cream | `#F6F4EF` | Page background |
| Charcoal | `#2B2B2B` | Body text |

### Typography
- **Display / Headings:** Cormorant Garamond — italic, weight 300–400. Use for H1, H2, logo, pull quotes.
- **Body / UI:** Jost — weight 200–400. Use for body copy, navigation, captions, labels.
- **Pairing rule:** Serif for emotion and hierarchy. Sans for readability and support.

### Logo
- Mark: Hammock arc with two teal anchor dots and dashed drop lines. White arc on dark backgrounds, deep teal on light.
- Wordmark: "Aerial Yoga" in Cormorant Garamond italic, large.
- Variants: Full colour on deep teal (primary) · Reversed white on primary teal · Deep teal on cream · Single colour black · Icon only (circle mark for favicon/avatar) · Horizontal lockup · Stacked lockup.

### Brand Feeling
- Mood: Calm, spacious, inviting
- Energy: Playful but grounded
- Not: Clinical, trendy, loud, intimidating
- Feels like: A warm studio you want to come back to

### Mission Statement
> "Playful movement. Powerful connection."

### Guiding Design Principle
> "The website should feel the way the classes feel — light, a bit playful, but grounded and trustworthy. Someone should land on it and feel immediately welcome, even if they've never tried aerial yoga before."

### Voice & Tone
- Speak like an encouraging friend, not a fitness instructor
- Always: Warm, honest, inclusive
- Never: Jargon, pressure, intimidation
- Audience: All levels welcome — especially complete beginners

## CLASSES

### Class 1 — Aerial Yoga for Beginners

**Schedule:** Tuesday · 7:30 – 8:30 pm
**Level:** Beginner — no experience needed

**Gif's Description (raw):**
"In the beginner class, we focus on building a strong foundation. It's not an intense workout — it's a gentle, steady practice that teaches you the basics of aerial movement. We work on foundational poses, coordination, and confidence, so you can make it part of your routine. As you grow more comfortable, you can build up and step into the next level class when you're ready."

**Website Copy (expanded):**
If you've ever been curious about aerial yoga but weren't sure where to start — this is it. Gif designed this class to remove every barrier. No experience, no special fitness level, no particular flexibility required. Just a willingness to try something different.

You'll learn how to use the hammock safely, move through the foundational poses, and begin to build the coordination and core strength that underpins everything else in aerial practice. The pace is steady and kind. Nobody is expected to get it perfect.

Most students are surprised by how quickly they settle in — and how good it feels.

**Who It's For:**
Complete beginners. Anyone returning after a break. People who are curious but nervous. Those looking for something gentle but with real physical benefit.

**Call to Action:**
"Your first class starts on Tuesday. Get in touch to reserve your spot — Gif will make sure you feel welcome from the moment you walk in."

### Class 2 — Improver Aerial

**Schedule:** Wednesday · 7:30 – 8:30 pm
**Level:** Improver — minimum 4 beginner classes recommended

**Gif's Description (raw):**
"Designed for students who have completed at least four beginner classes, this level bridges foundational skills with more intentional progression. We'll focus on refining technique, building core strength, and preparing you for more advanced sequences. This is your next step toward mastering aerial practice."

**Website Copy (expanded):**
You've found your footing. Now it's time to go further.

The Improver class is for students who've moved through the beginner foundations and are ready to bring more intention to their practice. Classes focus on technical refinement — the quality of movement, the engagement of the right muscles, the transitions between poses that start to feel seamless rather than effortful.

Core strength becomes more central here, as does the awareness needed to move safely into more complex sequences. This class builds the physical and mental groundwork for everything that comes next.

If you're not sure whether you're ready, ask Gif. She'll tell you honestly.

**Who It's For:**
Students who have completed at least 4 beginner sessions. Those who feel comfortable in the hammock and want to develop further. Anyone working toward more advanced aerial practice.

**Call to Action:**
"Ready to take the next step? Get in touch and Gif will confirm you're in the right place to move up."

### Class 3 — Aerial Hammock

**Schedule:** Tuesday · 6:30 – 7:30 pm
**Level:** All levels welcome

**Gif's Description (raw):**
"Experience a seamless blend of graceful movement and dance. This class integrates fluid transitions, rhythmic sequences, and the calming support of the hammock to let you express your creativity in the air."

**Website Copy (expanded):**
This is aerial yoga at its most expressive.

The Aerial Hammock class brings together movement, rhythm and the unique sensation of being held by the silk — and invites you to explore what's possible within that. Fluid transitions move from one shape to the next in a way that feels almost choreographed. Rhythmic sequences help you find flow rather than fighting for it.

It's the class where the practice starts to feel less like exercise and more like something else entirely — something that's harder to put into words, but that students come back to week after week.

Open to all levels, though a little comfort with the hammock helps.

**Who It's For:**
Anyone who wants to explore the more expressive, movement-led side of aerial yoga. Students at all levels who are comfortable in the hammock. Those drawn to dance, rhythm and creative movement.

**Call to Action:**
"Join us on Tuesday evening. Get in touch to book your place."

## FAQ

| Question | Answer |
|---|---|
| Do I need experience? | Not at all. The Beginners class is designed for complete newcomers. |
| What should I wear? | Fitted clothing that covers your armpits and the backs of your knees — the silk can pinch loose fabric. |
| Is it safe? | Yes. Gif guides every student through the hammock safely. Classes are small so she can give proper attention. |
| How high off the ground? | Not very — the hammock is low enough that you're never far from the floor. |
| Can I come if I'm not fit? | Absolutely. Aerial yoga builds fitness — it doesn't require it. |
| How do I pay? | Gif will confirm payment details when she gets back to you. Cash, Revolut and bank transfer all accepted. |
